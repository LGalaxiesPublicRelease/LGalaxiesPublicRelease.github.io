Project: Molecules in L-Galaxies

Status

Ongoing

Collaborators

Diederick Vroom, Rob Yates, Gijs Vermarien, Serena Viti

Description

We are implementing a model for molecule formation into the Yates+24 version of L-GALAXIES. This simulation already contains models for chemical element and dust formation. The next step is modelling the formation of (complex) organic molecules through bonding of chemical elements on the surfaces of dust grains in the interstellar medium (ISM). These molecules are the fundamental ingredients for life in the Universe. A neural-network-based emulator for molecule formation developed in Leiden will be implemented into L-GALAXIES. The results will be compared to current CO, H2O, and organic molecule observations from the ALMA and JWST telescopes, and provide crucial predictions for future observational surveys. This project will allow us to predict molecule abundances in simulated galaxies across all time and space, providing, among other things, an unprecedentedly detailed overview of when and where life could form in the Universe.
