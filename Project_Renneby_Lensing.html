Project: Lensing

Status

Submitted

Collaborators

Malin Renneby, Bruno Henriques, Stefan Hilbert, Dylan Nelson, Mark Vogelsberger, Raul Angulo, Volker Springel, Lars Hernquist

Description

We compare predictions for galaxy-galaxy lensing profiles and galaxy clustering from the Henrqiues+15 version of L-Galaxies and the IllustrisTNG suite, primarily TNG300, with observations from the KiDS+GAMA equatorial overlap and SDSS. Using four different selection functions for the lenses (stellar mass, stellar mass and group membership, stellar mass and isolation criteria, stellar mass and color) we find that this version of the SAM is disfavored by current data for stellar masses > 10^11M_solar. By decreasing the merger time for satellite galaxies as well as reducing the AGN accretion efficiency of the radio-mode in the SAM, we obtain better agreement, both for the lensing and the clustering signal at the high mass end.
