Project: Synthetic spectra for GCE

Status

Ongoing

Collaborators

Rob Yates, Aswin Vijayan

Description

L-Galaxies has come a long way in developing its Galactic Chemical Evolution (GCE) modelling. However, there is still a key difference between how metallicities are measured in L-Galaxies and in observations. In L-Galaxies (and most other simulations), gas phase oxygen abundances (O/H) are measured from the ratio of O and H masses produced by stars and SNe in the ISM. In observations, O/H is inferred from the emission lines emitted from HII regions. We address this discrepancy by coupling L-Galaxies (Yates+24 version) to the Synthesizer synthetic observables package in post processing, to generate synthetic emission-line spectra for our model galaxies. These will be used to measure O/H in the ISM exactly as observers do, and study the impact this (and dust attenuation) has on e.g. the inferred mass - metallicity relation at high and low redshift.
