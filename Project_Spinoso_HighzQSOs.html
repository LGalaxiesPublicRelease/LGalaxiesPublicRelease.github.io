Description

We use L-GalaxiesBH and the model for the emission of high-z accreting BHs in order to study how the environment of z>7-8 QSOs, as observed by the JWST, can help us to disentangle the origin of the first super-massive BHs. In particular, we connect the UV emission of accreting BHs to the properties of their local environments and formation scenarios (i.e. heavy/light seeding). We then produce mock data of a typical JWST observation and explore the possibility that different formation channels of high-z black holes leave an imprint on the spectro-photometric properties of high-z QSOs as well as on their close environment.
