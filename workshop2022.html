Workshop 2022

Basic information

We are very pleased to announce the final dates for the L-Galaxies workshop 2022:


Date: 16th & 17th November 2022
Location: Enterprise Hub @ University of Hertfordshire, UK
Hosted by: The Centre for Astrophysics Research (CAR)


The workshop will include:
(a) presentations on the latest L-Galaxies code developments and results from various groups,
(b) discussions about future plans for the model and possible collaborations, and
(c) a "coding session" to get new users up-and-running with the latest public version of the code, L-Galaxies 2020.


If you are interested in attending, either in-person or virtually, please contact the main organiser (Rob Yates) at:
r.yates3 -at- herts.ac.uk

Venue

The University of Hertfordshire is situated in the town of Hatfield.
It is divided into two main campuses, the College Lane campus and the De Havilland campus. The Enterprise Hub (where the workshop will be held) is on the de Havilland campus.
Information on how to reach the De Havilland campus (including maps and transport info) is provided here: Getting to the de Havilland campus
Additional travel and accommodation info is provided below.

Travel

Arriving by train
Hatfield [HAT] and St Albans [SAC] stations are served by regular trains from Kings Cross [KGX] station, London. The journey normally takes 20-25 mins.
Tickets can be purchased from e.g. National Rail or Trainline
The regional "UNO bus" (number 602) from either station will then take you direct to the de Havilland campus. This normally takes around 15-20 mins.
Some other UNO buses also link Hatfield/St Albans to the de Havilland campus. See here: UNO bus

Unfortunately, public transport in the UK isn't the cheapest, so prepare to pay a little more than expected!
The LOC will also keep a close eye on any planned bus or train strikes this November, and will keep you informed accordingly.
