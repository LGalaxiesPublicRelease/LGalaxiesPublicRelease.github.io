Project: Hot gas profiles

Status

Ongoing

Collaborators

Jian Fu, Wenxin Zhong, Prateek Sharma, Xi Kang, Shiyin Shen

Description

In current L-Galaxies models, the hot gas in the halo is assumed to follow an isothermal radial profile, which can cause an over-prediction of the X-ray luminosity. Therefore, inspired by recent observations from Chandra and the model work by Sharma et al. 2012, we plan to adopt a more physical radial distribution for the hot gas component in L-Galaxies, based on hydrostatic and thermal considerations. We will use the new profiles to re-calculate the cooling radius, cooling rate, and ram pressure stripping for the hot gas component. The new model will be analysed to see if it matches the scaling relations for Lx-Tx and Lx-Mvir from observations. The model will also be used to give predictions for future X-ray surveys of the CGM.
